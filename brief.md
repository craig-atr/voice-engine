# Client Brief

**Client:** Craig Howard — owner & certified laser technician, Atomic Tattoo Removal (Wrentham, MA)
**Specialist requested:** A folder-based ghostwriter that drafts my customer and business email in my own voice.

**What's broken.** I run a tattoo removal studio solo. Almost every day I'm answering the same kinds of email: pricing and "how many sessions" questions from new customers, healing and aftercare questions, "is this normal" photos, rescheduling, plus a steady stream of business email — ad proofs, sponsorships, vendors, invoices. The writing is genuinely mine and it matters (people are nervous about a laser near their skin, and my replies have to sound calm, clear, and human). But it gets repetitive and eats my evenings. When I hand it to an AI to go faster, the drafts come back stiff and corporate — long-winded, over-polished, full of "I hope this email finds you well." That's not how I write. I write short. So I rewrite the whole thing, and the AI saved me nothing.

**What I've tried.** Generic prompts like "write a friendly reply." Pasting one old email as an example. It gets closer but still misses me — my brevity, the way I explain clinical things in plain parentheses, my numbered options, my quick "Let me know." And nothing carries over between sessions.

**What I need.** A specialist that holds *my* voice — pulled from my own sent email — and drafts customer-care and B2B replies that sound like me, and gets sharper as I log corrections. Once the routine email drafts itself, I finally have room for the marketing I never get to (review requests, promos, a customer newsletter).

**Desired output.** First drafts I can send in under two minutes of editing. **Success:** a regular customer couldn't tell my reply from the system's.
